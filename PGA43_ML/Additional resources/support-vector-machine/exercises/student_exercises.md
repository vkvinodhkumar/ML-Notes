# Exercises and Interview Questions

## Guided exercises
1. Derive point-to-hyperplane distance.
2. Prove maximum-margin equivalence.
3. Compute hinge loss for correct, margin-violating, and misclassified points.
4. Compare regularization values in the scratch model.
5. Compare scaled and unscaled SVM.
6. Plot linear margins on two-dimensional data.
7. Tune `C` logarithmically.
8. Tune `C` and `gamma` jointly.
9. Inspect support-vector fraction.
10. Evaluate an imbalanced dataset.
11. Calibrate probabilities and compare Brier score.
12. Benchmark prediction latency against support-vector count.

## Challenges
Implement stochastic subgradient descent, sample weights, one-vs-rest multiclass, a simple kernel dual solver, early stopping, and runtime benchmarks.

## Interview questions
What defines a support vector? Why maximum margin? What do `C` and `gamma` control? Why scale? Hinge versus log loss? Why are scores not probabilities? When is a linear kernel preferable? Why can many support vectors be problematic? How does multiclass work? Why is nonlinear SVM difficult at scale?
