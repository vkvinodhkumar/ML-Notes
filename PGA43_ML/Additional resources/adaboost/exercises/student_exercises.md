# Exercises and Interview Questions

## Exercises
1. Compute one boosting round manually.
2. Derive learner contribution for errors 0.1, 0.25, and 0.49.
3. Show how observation emphasis changes after correct and incorrect predictions.
4. Plot the concentration of emphasis by round.
5. Compare stumps with depth-two trees.
6. Tune learning rate and estimator count jointly.
7. Inject label noise and measure degradation.
8. Compare AdaBoost with Random Forest and Gradient Boosting.
9. Plot margin distributions.
10. Evaluate an imbalanced target using macro F1 and balanced accuracy.

## Interview questions
Why must a weak learner beat chance? Why exponential loss? What happens when weighted error is nearly zero? How does AdaBoost differ from Gradient Boosting? Why is it noise-sensitive? What does learning rate control? Why are stumps common? Can AdaBoost overfit? How are probabilities produced? What should be monitored in production?
