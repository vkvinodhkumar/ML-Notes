# Student Exercises

## Foundation
1. Explain the difference between an OLS coefficient, a Pearson correlation, and a standardized coefficient.
2. Identify three places where data leakage could occur in this workflow.
3. Explain why a p-value does not measure practical importance.

## Diagnostics
4. Use residual plots and RESET to evaluate functional form.
5. Compare conventional and HC3 standard errors.
6. Find the five most influential observations and write an audit plan.
7. Explain why high VIF can coexist with good predictions.

## Modeling
8. Add selected spline or polynomial terms and validate them.
9. Fit a count-model sensitivity analysis.
10. Bootstrap the difference in RMSE between the two best candidates.
11. Build conformal prediction intervals and assess coverage by ring band.

## Communication
12. Produce a model card for a nontechnical stakeholder.
13. Write a paragraph that explicitly avoids causal claims.
14. Recommend whether this model should be deployed and list the required monitoring controls.
